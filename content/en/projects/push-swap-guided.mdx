## Project idea

Think of two small piles of number cards.

The first pile is called `a`. It starts full of messy numbers. The second pile
is called `b`. It starts empty. Your job is to move the cards around until pile
`a` is cleanly sorted from smallest to biggest.

Your program does not move the cards by hand. It prints little move words, and
the checker follows those words.

<Callout type="tip" title="The simple mental model">
Your program says the moves. The checker does the moves. If the checker ends
with pile `a` sorted and pile `b` empty, you win.
</Callout>

## The only moves

These are the only words your program is allowed to print. Each word is a tiny
instruction for the checker:

<Callout type="note" title="How to read the words">
`a` means pile `a`. `b` means pile `b`. `s` means swap. `p` means push. `r`
means rotate. So `sa` means swap pile `a`, and `pb` means push one number from
pile `a` to pile `b`.
</Callout>

  items={[

First make the moves trustworthy. Sorting becomes much less scary after that.
